# Distilled mechanical cores of "9 Profitable Trading Strategies" (R. Sadowski / HumbleTraders PDF).
# Strategy 1's fundamental/COT overlay is not backtestable; reduced to its mechanical core.
# Run per asset class: --ideas-file this file --timeframe {4h|1d} --instruments <basket> --reprocess-ideas
PDF-1 Momentum reversal at exhaustion: after a sustained directional move, when both a slow and a fast stochastic oscillator sit in overbought territory while price trades at the top of its recent multi-week range (or oversold at the bottom), the late-chasing crowd is fully positioned and the move reverses; fade the extreme with an ATR stop beyond the range edge and exit at the range midpoint or on momentum normalizing.
PDF-2 Triple moving average crossover: buy when a 20-period average crosses above a 60-period average and price holds beyond a 100-period trend filter; sell/short on the reverse cross; exit when the 20/60 cross back. Trend followers harvest the slow repricing that range traders fight; the 100-period filter suppresses whipsaw entries in ranges.
PDF-3 Heikin-Ashi exhaustion reversal: compute Heikin-Ashi candles; after a one-sided run, two consecutive opposite-color HA candles mark exhaustion; confirm with a stochastic (14,7,3) at the opposite extreme (long requires stochastic overbought collapse territory per the source: long after two red HA candles with stochastic above 70, short after two green with stochastic below 30); enter on a stop beyond the second reversal candle's extreme with an ATR stop.
PDF-4 Swing continuation out of compression: within an established trend, a correction shows as many overlapping narrow bars going nowhere; trapped countertrend traders and resting stops fuel the resumption; enter when price breaks the compression range in the trend direction, stop at the opposite extreme of the correction.
PDF-5 Candlestick reversal patterns: engulfing bars, hammers, and long-shadow bars at swing extremes mark forced repositioning; enter at the next bar's open in the pattern direction with the stop at the pattern bar's extreme, trail the stop with the trend, time-exit if nothing happens.
PDF-6 Role reversal pullback: when price breaks above a multi-week resistance level, the level flips to support as trapped shorts and missed-out buyers defend it; buy the first pullback into the broken level zone in an uptrend (short the mirror in a downtrend), stop beyond the zone, target the prior swing extension.
PDF-7 Bollinger band squeeze: when the 20-period 2-sigma band width contracts into its lowest decile of the past months, volatility sellers are complacent and a regime shift is near; enter in the direction of the first full candle closing beyond the 20-period mid-band, stop at the prior candle's extreme, ride until the opposite band touch or band re-expansion fades.
PDF-8 Narrow range breakout: a bar whose range is the narrowest of the last 4-7 bars (NR4/NR7) signals coiled volatility; buy a break of its high or sell a break of its low with the stop at the other side of the narrow bar; volatility cycles from contraction to expansion so the breakout has follow-through.
PDF-9 Two-period RSI momentum: as written in the source, buy when RSI(2) rises above 90 and sell/short when RSI(2) falls below 10, closing on the opposite signal; this bets that very short-term momentum extremes mark initiative flow that persists rather than mean-reverts.
