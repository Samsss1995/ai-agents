# Combination / regime-gated ideas: grid trading + SMA/EMA/stochRSI/Bollinger confluence.
# Multi-instrument specs are now judged on the equal-weight PORTFOLIO of the class basket.
# Run per class via: sh src/scripts/run_ideas_cycles.sh src/data/research_factory/ideas_combos.txt
COMBO-1 Regime-gated grid: when the Bollinger (20,2) band width sits in its lowest quartile of the trailing year and price is within one band-width of the 20-SMA, the market is range-bound and mean reversion pays; run a symmetric grid that scales into longs each ATR-fraction step below the SMA and exits each leg at the SMA or one step in profit, mirrored with shorts above the SMA. Hard kill: flatten everything and stand down when price closes beyond 2.5 sigma or band width leaves the bottom quartile - trends destroy grids, so the regime gate and kill switch are the strategy. Requires pyramiding: costs_to_model exclusive_orders false.
COMBO-2 StochRSI dip-buy in uptrend: long-only while price holds above the 200-SMA; buy when stochRSI(14) drops below 0.2 then turns up, exit at the 20-SMA touch or stochRSI above 0.8, ATR stop below the dip low; in downtrends (below 200-SMA) take only the mirrored short on stochRSI above 0.8 turning down. Fear-driven dip sellers in established trends hand systematic buyers their capitulation; the trend filter is what the naked oscillator strategies tested earlier lacked.
COMBO-3 Bollinger band reversion with EMA200 gate: when price closes below the lower Bollinger band (20,2) while above the 200-EMA, the move is statistical overshoot inside an uptrend, not regime change; buy and exit at the mid-band with an ATR stop; mirror with shorts at the upper band below the 200-EMA. The trend gate removes the catastrophic counter-trend fades that killed the raw exhaustion strategies.
COMBO-4 EMA trend with stochRSI pullback timing: trade only when EMA50 is above EMA200 (or mirrored below); enter long when stochRSI crosses up from below 0.3 - a pullback exhausting inside an uptrend; exit on a close below the EMA50 or stochRSI above 0.9; ATR stop. Confluence of regime and timing should raise per-trade quality versus either indicator alone.
COMBO-5 Volatility-targeted EMA cross: take EMA20/EMA100 cross direction but size every position so the ATR-implied risk equals a constant fraction of equity (smaller size in turbulence, larger in calm); exit on the opposite cross. Vol targeting is the managed-futures trick that converts a fat-tailed trend P&L into a higher Sharpe one; the signal stays identical to the crossover already tested, isolating the sizing effect.
COMBO-6 Squeeze breakout with trend direction gate: when Bollinger band width is in its lowest decile, take the first full candle close beyond the 20-SMA ONLY in the direction of the 200-EMA slope, stop at the prior candle extreme, trail at the mid-band. The undirected squeeze tested earlier bought false breakouts against the prevailing trend; gating direction should remove the worst half of its trades.
COMBO-7 Anti-grid trend pyramid: while EMA50 is above EMA200, open an initial long on a 20-SMA reclaim and add one equal unit each time price advances a full ATR above the last add, maximum four units, trailing the whole stack two ATR below the highest close; mirrored short stack in downtrends. Pyramiding converts trend persistence into convexity - small repeated losses, occasional large compounding wins. Requires pyramiding: costs_to_model exclusive_orders false.
COMBO-8 Triple-confluence extreme fade: fade only when three independent gauges agree the move is stretched - stochRSI beyond 0.9 (or below 0.1), close outside the Bollinger band, and distance from the 50-EMA in its top decile of the trailing year; exit at the band midline, ATR stop beyond the extreme. Requiring triple confluence cuts the trade count to genuine outliers where mean reversion has the strongest base rate.
