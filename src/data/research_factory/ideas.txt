Volatility compression precedes expansion: when realized volatility on crypto majors falls below its 20th percentile for several sessions, breakouts from the compressed range carry follow-through because market makers widen quotes and stops cluster outside the range. Trade the breakout with ATR stops, no trades during normal volatility.
Failed breakout mean reversion: when price on a crypto major pierces a multi-day high by less than half an ATR and closes back inside the range within a few bars, trapped breakout buyers must unwind, pushing price toward the range midpoint. Short the failure with a stop above the false-break high.
Session behavior: crypto majors tend to range during Asian hours and trend during US hours; suppress breakout entries outside US session hours and require above-median volume confirmation, exiting all positions before the Asian session open.
Order-flow imbalance proxy on crypto majors (true OBI needs L2 book history we do not have): persistent aggressor imbalance shows up in OHLCV as bars closing near the top of their range on above-median volume while range stays contained - passive sellers absorbing then failing. When a rolling imbalance score (close-location-value weighted by relative volume) stays one-sided for several bars and price clears the absorption range, enter with the imbalance; exit when the score flips to neutral.
