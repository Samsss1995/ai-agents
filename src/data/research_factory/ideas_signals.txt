Extreme funding fade: when the hourly FundingRate on a crypto major sits in its top few percent of the trailing month for several consecutive hours, longs are crowded and paying heavily to stay in; the marginal buyer is exhausted and liquidation cascades sit below. Short the exhaustion when price momentum stalls (price below its short EMA), cover when funding normalizes toward its monthly median or after a fixed holding period. ATR stop above recent highs. Symmetric long side on extreme negative funding.
Funding regime persistence: sustained moderately positive funding with rising price reflects healthy levered demand, not exhaustion - the carry crowd keeps bid. Stay long while FundingRate holds above its trailing median but below crowding extremes and price holds above a slow moving average; exit when funding spikes into the extreme decile (crowding) or drops below median (demand gone). Few trades, multi-day holds, wide ATR stop.
Funding-price divergence: when price makes a new multi-week high but FundingRate is falling versus its own recent average, the move is spot-driven and shorts are covering rather than longs chasing - continuation is more likely; when a new high comes with sharply rising funding, the move is leverage-driven and fragile - fade it. Trade the divergence direction with ATR stops and time-based exits, requiring the divergence to persist several bars before entry.
